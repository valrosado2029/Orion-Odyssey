Safety is our top priority at all times.

In order to ensure the safety of all passengers and crew members, we have implemented strict safety procedures that must be followed before, during, and after each flight. These procedures include:


  1. Pre-flight Safety Briefing: Before the flight, all passengers will attend a pre-flight safety briefing where they will be instructed on emergency procedures, including how to use safety equipment, how to evacuate the spacecraft, and how to handle emergency situations.

  2. Personal Protective Equipment: All passengers will be required to wear personal protective equipment (PPE) during the flight, including a helmet, pressure suit, and gloves. This equipment is designed to protect passengers from the vacuum of space and other hazards that may be encountered during the flight.

  3. Launch and Landing Procedures: Our launch and landing procedures are designed to minimize risk to passengers and crew members. During launch, passengers will be securely strapped into their seats, and the spacecraft will be monitored closely to ensure safe ascent. During landing, the spacecraft will be slowed by parachutes and other safety mechanisms to ensure a safe and gentle landing.

  4. Emergency Procedures: In the event of an emergency, our crew members are trained to quickly and safely respond to any situation. Emergency procedures include procedures for fire, loss of pressure, and other potential hazards.
