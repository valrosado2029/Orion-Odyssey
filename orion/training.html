Our Training Process

Physical fitness training

Astronauts need to be in top physical condition to withstand the physical demands of space travel. This involves a rigorous fitness program that includes cardiovascular training, strength training, and flexibility exercises.

Survival training

Astronauts must learn survival skills in case of an emergency situation during launch, in space, or upon reentry. This includes water survival, fire suppression, and wilderness survival.

Medical training

Astronauts must be trained in basic medical procedures as well as how to deal with medical issues in space, such as motion sickness and changes in blood pressure.

Team-Building training

Astronauts have to work closely with their crewmates and ground support team during a space mission, so team-building and communication training is essential to ensure effective teamwork and successful mission completion.

Mental Health training

Astronauts must be mentally prepared for the isolation and stress of space travel. Mental health training includes stress management techniques, coping strategies, and psychological support.

Pre-launch training

Prior to launch, astronauts undergo final preparation, including spacecraft checkout, equipment testing, and last-minute training on emergency procedures.
